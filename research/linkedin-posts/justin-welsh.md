## Post 1 31st March 2026

The current world is a noisy mess.

And SO many people are struggling.

I'm certainly not immune to the chaos and the amount of stuff pulling at our attention.

But, when I need to reground myself, I always come back to the same four things:

1. Find time to think clearly.

Rather than scrolling or consuming content, find 2-3 hours to write something meaningful.

Just turn my thoughts into words on a page.

2. Get some deep, peaceful sleep.

Get in bed by 9 p.m. and clear my alarm with the goal of sleeping until 5:30 a.m. or 6.

3. Move slowly, but with intention.

Find one project I love working on, and take pride in making it objectively better.

4. Live and enjoy a quieter life.

Put guardrails on computer time, invite friends over to our house, and cook great food together.

These are my four.
Your four might be different.
And that's OK.

The point is that all of the nonsense that's infiltrating your brain from every angle is mostly just that...nonsense.

Unplug, relax, and get refocused on the things that matter.

That's how I like to live.

If you're interested in that kind of lifestyle, I write a short Saturday essay each week to help people.

One story or lesson on rethinking how you work, earn, and live.

## Post 2 31st March 2026

You only capture about 25% of the value you create at work.

That's been the business "formula" forever.

Here's an example:

You're a salesperson with a quota of $1M dollars per year.

What's your take-home pay on hitting that? Probably ~$250K.

The other $750K is reinvested into the business to help it continue to grow.

That's not unfair at all. 

After all, they're the ones who gave you the job, the collateral, the desk, the equipment, and everything else in the first place.

But that has to itch a little, no? 
How do you capture that extra $750K?

You need to own the assets that the company currently gives you:

- A product or service
- A distribution channel

The easiest way to do this is to create your own distribution channel.

For example, I can singlehandedly create 100x more impressions for my business on social media than a typical startup.

That's how important these channels are.

Once you create distribution? 

Talk to people.
Understand the problem they need solved.
Build a product or service to solve it.
Sell it to them.

Ta-da. You've got distribution and a product.

You can now capture 100% of the value you create.

Hard as hell.
Completely worth it.

That's how I like to build, earn, and live.

## Post 3 30th March 2026

The world is going to change rapidly in the next 10 years.

And I fear that a lot of people aren't prepared for what's coming.

A steep learning curve at work.
A disruption to the global job market.
A change in how the average person earns.

Here's the big problem with that:

Most people's 10-year plan is just their current year's plan repeated 10 times.

And that's not going to cut it.

So, consider preparing.

- Reduce your expenses
- Find a way to add a second income stream
- Study what's going on with AI in the workplace

Because I'd hate for you to figure out that the biggest financial decision impacting your life this decade will be made by your employer during a meeting you're not invited to.

And you never see it coming.
