## Post 1 31st March 2026

99% of marriage advice is noise.

(The answer is embarrassingly simple.)

Compatibility tests. 
Love languages. 
Communication frameworks. 
Date night schedules.

None of it matters if you don't genuinely like being around each other.

The couples who last aren't the ones who "work on their marriage" the hardest.

They're the ones who would choose each other's company over almost anyone else's.

On a random Tuesday. With nothing planned.

No kids to manage. 
No dinner reservation. 
No agenda.

Not the vacations. 
Not the milestones. 
Not the holidays.

The ordinary moments in between.

A great marriage isn't built on grand gestures.

It's built on genuinely enjoying the person sitting next to you.

The laughing at nothing. 
The comfortable silence. 
The inside jokes no one else would understand.

If you have that, you have 99% of it figured out.

## Post 2 31st March 2026

Most people in your life would leave tomorrow. 

(If you stopped being useful.)

That's not a comfortable thought.

But sit with it for a second.

Think about the people who call when they need something. 

The ones who show up when life is fun, convenient, or valuable.

Now think about who would still be there if all of that disappeared.

No job. 
No status. 
No connections to offer. 
No fun plans.
Just you.

Here's the truth:
Most of your friendships are built on exchange, not loyalty.

Your real friends are the ones who show up when you have nothing to offer in return.

Those people are rare. 

And most people go their entire lives without knowing who they are.

Find them. 
Cherish them. 
Be that person for someone else.
