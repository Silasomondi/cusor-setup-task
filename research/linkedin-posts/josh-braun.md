# Josh Braun - LinkedIn Posts

## Post 1 1st April 2026

A prospect says:

“I’m busy right now.”

And the rep says:

“I’ll make it quick. The reason for my call is…”

That’s usually the moment the call dies.

Why?

Because you’re not listening.

You’re explaining.

The moment someone says they’re busy, they’re giving you information.

Not an objection to overcome.

A signal to notice.

The more you explain,
the less people trust you.

Explaining feels like pushing.

And when people feel the push,
they push back.

Instead, label the moment.

Lean back.

“Sounds like I caught you in the middle of something.”

Pause.

Let them respond.

Now they feel heard.

Not handled.

Sometimes they’ll say:

“Yeah, I’m heading into a meeting.”

Great.

Other times they’ll say:

“No, I’ve got a minute.”

Trust starts when people feel understood.

Not when they feel managed.

Don’t explain your way past resistance.

Name what’s happening.

Then lean back.

## Post 2 31st March 2026

This made me better at sales.

Perhaps it will make you better too.

My success depended on how many uncomfortable conversations I had with the right people.

There were times when I didn’t make many cold calls.

I hid behind emails.

I ended up not doing well.

When I picked up the phone consistently, I had more uncomfortable conversations with my ideal clients.

The more conversations I had, the more money I made.

Your success depends on how many uncomfortable conversations you’re willing to have.

The phone isn’t a cactus.

Pick it up.
