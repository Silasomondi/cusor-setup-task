# 1. Overview

This playbook outlines a proven system for building a LinkedIn organic content engine for B2B SaaS using insights from top creators and practitioners.

# 2. Core Strategy

## 2.1 Consistency Over Virality

Post consistently to build trust and audience familiarity.

“Prioritize consistent posting to build trust and authority”  
(Source: Alex Hormozi, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=91D5hjMEADg)

“Consistency compounds results over time”  
(Source: Lara Acosta, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Foh1r-TdnTk)

---

## 2.2 Hook-Driven Content

Start every post with a strong hook.

“The fix isn’t posting more… it’s having something different to say”  
(Source: Katelyn Bourgoin, LinkedIn Post, 31st March 2026)

Hooks should:
- Create curiosity
- Challenge assumptions
- Stop scrolling

---

## 2.3 Skimmable Formatting

Use:
- One sentence per line
- Short paragraphs
- White space

This increases readability and engagement  
(Source: Insights.md)

---

## 2.4 Value-First Content

Focus on:
- Education
- Practical advice
- Real experiences

“Educational content attracts engaged viewers and leads”  
(Source: Alex Hormozi, YouTube)

---

## 2.5 Build Distribution First

“You need to create your own distribution channel”  
(Source: Justin Welsh, LinkedIn Post, 31st March 2026)

LinkedIn = primary distribution engine

---

## 2.6 Repurpose Content

“Repurpose long-form content into smaller snippets”  
(Source: Alex Hormozi, YouTube)

“Repurpose across platforms for maximum reach”  
(Source: Lara Acosta, YouTube)

---

## 2.7 Engage to Grow

“Spend 15 minutes daily commenting on posts”  
(Source: Austin Belcak, LinkedIn Post, 31st March 2026)

# 3. Content Execution Framework (SOP)

## Step 1: Define Content Pillars
- Industry insights
- Personal experiences
- Tactical advice

---

## Step 2: Create Daily/Weekly Posts

Use structure:
1. Hook
2. Story or insight
3. Lesson
4. CTA

---

## Step 3: Publish Consistently
- 3–5 posts per week

---

## Step 4: Engage Daily
- Comment
- Reply
- Network

---

## Step 5: Repurpose Content
- LinkedIn → Twitter → YouTube Shorts

---

## Step 6: Build Funnel

“LinkedIn → Newsletter → Paid product”  
(Source: Justin Welsh, YouTube)

# 4. Where Experts Disagree

## 4.1 Posting Frequency

**Lara Acosta:**
- Daily posting builds growth  
(Source: YouTube)

**Katelyn Bourgoin:**
- Messaging matters more than frequency  
(Source: LinkedIn, 31st March 2026)

**My Position:**
Post 3–5 times weekly  
Reason:
Balances quality and consistency

---

## 4.2 Content Style

**Sahil Bloom:**
- Deep storytelling  
(Source: LinkedIn, 31st March 2026)

**Alex Hormozi:**
- Direct, blunt content  
(Source: LinkedIn, 30th March 2026)

**My Position:**
Blend storytelling + direct insights  
Reason:
Maximizes engagement + clarity

---

## 4.3 Growth Strategy

**Austin Belcak:**
- Networking + engagement  
(Source: LinkedIn, 31st March 2026)

**Justin Welsh:**
- Content-first growth  
(Source: LinkedIn, 31st March 2026)

Content + engagement combined  
Reason:
Content attracts, engagement converts

# 6. My Original Ideas

## Idea 1: Content Feedback Loop System
After each post:
- Track performance
- Improve next post

Why it works:
- Continuous improvement  
(Source inspiration: Ross Simmonds feedback loop)

---

## Idea 2: Micro-Story Series

Create weekly story threads:
- “Lessons from X”
- “Mistakes I made”

Why it works:
- Builds anticipation
- Improves retention

---

## Idea 3: Audience Mining

Use comments as content ideas

Why it works:
- Direct audience insight
- Higher relevance

# 10. Final Insight

Most advice online focuses on posting more content.

However, the real leverage comes from:
- Clear positioning
- Strong messaging
- Consistent distribution

Content does not fail because of the algorithm.

It fails because:
- It is not differentiated
- It does not resonate
- It lacks clarity

This playbook prioritizes clarity and consistency over volume.
